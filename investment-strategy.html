Final Notes

Risk & Conclusions

Key Conclusions
01

Without additional capital, both $15M and $10M targets exceed the 2-year constraint under the 25% deployment rule.

02

Injecting capital at the Phase 2 transition significantly compresses the timeline and makes both 2-year targets achievable.

03

Early-stage scaling is the highest-leverage decision. The Phase 1 → Phase 2 transition carries the most impact on total timeline.

Risk Considerations
Sustaining 1.5% daily returns is a highly aggressive assumption; real-world results may vary significantly.
Growth is directly tied to capital deployment percentage, return consistency, and compounding duration.
The 25% post-$1M constraint is by design — a risk management measure, not a limitation.

Strategic lever: Increasing deployable capital at Phase 2 entry is the single most effective action to achieve either target within the 2-year benchmark. Both scenarios require an injection of $1.6M–$2.8M above organic Phase 1 output.
